Bar Operations Manual

Introduction

The following document contains three levels of classic cocktails which one would expect to be able to make in any bar of quality.

Please study Level One and the Minimalist and Maximalist drinks first in order to be prepared to bartend. Levels Two and Three may be studied and completed at your own pace.

Level One Cocktails

These classics will be ordered often. Master them.

You must know all these drinks in order to bartend. You must complete a pass/fail exam of 5 randomly selected drinks, and must make each completely correct and to quality standards.

Aperol Spritz

  • 3 oz Prosseco
  • 2 oz Aperol
  • 1 oz soda water
Build / Ice filled wine glass / Orange Peel

Level Two Cocktails

These drinks are a little more advanced, some require a developed palatte. If someone orders one of these, they expect flawless execution.

In order to be allowed to engage in "Dealer's Choice", one must pass another 5 drink exam in this level.

Airmail

  • 1.5 oz aged rum
  • 0.75 oz honey syrup
  • 0.75 oz lime juice
Shake / Coupe / Top with sparkling wine / Lime wedge

Level Three Cocktails

These are wonderful and strange libations, sure to satisfy the most discerning and adventurous guests.

Another exam of 5 random drinks will be administered in order to write new drinks for the bar.

Amaretto Sour

  • 1.5 oz Amaretto
  • 0.75 oz bourbon
  • 0.75 oz lemon juice
  • 1 bar spoon demarara syrup
  • 0.5 oz egg white
Reverse dry shake / Rocks / Lemon wedge and brandied cherry
